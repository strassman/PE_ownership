What is Private Equity in Healthcare?

Private equity (PE) firms are investment companies that buy businesses, cut costs to increase profits, then sell them — usually within 5–7 years. They've been buying up healthcare practices at a rapid pace since the 2010s.

When a PE firm buys a vet clinic, dental office, or doctor's practice, their goal is profit — not patient care. Studies show PE-owned practices tend to charge higher prices, see more patients per hour, use more upselling tactics, and have higher staff turnover.

Real examples in your area:
KKR owns Heartland Dental (1,600+ locations) · Mars Inc. owns Banfield, VCA & BluePearl · Gryphon Investors owns Bright Now! Dental

This doesn't mean every PE-backed practice is bad — but you deserve to know who owns where you get your care, so you can make an informed choice.
